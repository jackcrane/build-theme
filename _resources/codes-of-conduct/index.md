This page outlines the expectations for behavior and participation in the SLU OSS community. It is a living document, and we welcome feedback and suggestions for improvement. All document history is tracked in Git, and audits and suggestions are welcome.

## Community

**Scope**

This Code of Conduct applies to all participation in this platform, including but not limited to:
- Submitting content
- Reviewing content
- Commenting or discussion
- Direct or indirect communication through platform features

All users are expected to follow this code at all times.

**Expected Behavior**

Participants must:
- Act in good faith and with professional intent
- Engage with ideas, not attack individuals
- Provide constructive, relevant, and honest contributions
- Respect differing viewpoints and levels of experience
- Communicate clearly and without hostility

**Unacceptable Behavior**

The following are not permitted:
- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Personal attacks, insults, or inflammatory language
- Dismissive or non-constructive criticism
- Spam, self-promotion unrelated to the discussion, or manipulation of visibility
- Misrepresentation of work, credentials, or intent
- Plagiarism or knowingly submitting false or misleading information
- Attempts to exploit, disrupt, or degrade the platform

**Content Standards**

All contributions must:
- Be original or properly attributed
- Be technically honest and not intentionally misleading
- Avoid unnecessary hostility or sarcasm that undermines discussion
- Stay relevant to the topic or purpose of the platform

**Enforcement**

Violations may result in:
- Content removal
- Temporary or permanent suspension of participation
- Revocation of publishing or reviewing privileges

Enforcement decisions are made at the discretion of platform administrators and may occur without prior warning.

**Reporting**

If you encounter behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, report it through the designated platform channels. Include sufficient context for review. Violations can be reported to oss@slu.edu.

Participation in this platform is a privilege, not a right. Continued access is contingent on adherence to this Code of Conduct.

## Reviewers

**Scope**
- Applies to all individuals evaluating, commenting on, or making decisions about submitted work
- Covers formal reviews, informal feedback, and any reviewer-author interaction

**Core Responsibility**
- Reviewers are entrusted with maintaining the quality, integrity, and fairness of the platform
- Decisions and feedback must be based solely on the work, not the author

**Expected Behavior**
- Evaluate submissions objectively and consistently
- Provide clear, specific, and actionable feedback
- Justify decisions with reasoning tied to the content
- Maintain a professional and respectful tone at all times
- Acknowledge strengths as well as weaknesses where applicable
- Stay within your area of competence; defer when necessary

**Unacceptable Behavior**
- Personal attacks, condescension, or dismissive language
- Biased evaluation based on identity, background, or affiliation
- Superficial or unreasoned judgments (e.g., “this is bad” without explanation)
- Gatekeeping behavior that discourages legitimate contributions
- Using the review process to promote personal views unrelated to the work

**Conflict of Interest**
- Do not review work where you have a personal, professional, or financial conflict
- If a conflict exists or is suspected, recuse yourself immediately
- Do not attempt to influence the review outcome outside the formal process

**Quality Standards**
- Reviews must be:
  - Specific: reference concrete parts of the work
  - Constructive: offer improvements where possible
  - Proportionate: match depth of critique to the complexity of the work
- Avoid unnecessary verbosity or irrelevant commentary

**Timeliness**
- Complete reviews within the expected timeframe
- Communicate promptly if delays occur

**Enforcement**
- Violations may result in:
  - Removal of reviewer privileges
  - Suspension or removal from the platform
- Repeated low-quality or negligent reviews may be treated as violations

**Final Note**
- The role of a reviewer is to elevate the quality of work on the platform, not to obstruct it
- Exercise judgment with fairness, clarity, and accountability

## Authors

**Scope**
- Applies to all individuals submitting content to the platform
- Covers initial submissions, revisions, and any communication with reviewers or administrators

**Core Responsibility**
- Authors are responsible for the integrity, originality, and clarity of their work
- Submissions should aim to contribute meaningful insight, not just surface-level output

**Expected Behavior**
- Submit work that is honest, original, and properly attributed
- Clearly communicate ideas, assumptions, and limitations
- Respond to feedback professionally and in good faith
- Revise work thoughtfully when requested, addressing feedback directly
- Stay within the bounds of topics appropriate to the platform

**Unacceptable Behavior**
- Plagiarism or failure to properly attribute sources
- Submitting misleading, fabricated, or intentionally deceptive content
- Hostile, dismissive, or non-cooperative responses to feedback
- Spamming submissions or attempting to game acceptance processes
- Submitting low-effort or incomplete work with no intent to improve
- Misrepresenting authorship, credentials, or contributions

**Attribution and Sources**
- Credit all external ideas, code, or content appropriately
- Clearly distinguish original contributions from referenced material
- Do not present others’ work as your own under any circumstances

**Revision Standards**
- Address reviewer feedback directly and substantively
- Do not ignore major critiques without justification
- If disagreeing with feedback, provide clear and reasoned explanations

**Quality Standards**
- Submissions should be:
  - Technically accurate to the best of your knowledge
  - Structured and readable
  - Sufficiently complete to stand on their own
- Avoid unnecessary filler or vague explanations

**Collaboration and Communication**
- Engage respectfully with reviewers and platform staff
- Keep communication focused, relevant, and professional
- Do not attempt to bypass or pressure the review process

**Enforcement**
- Violations may result in:
  - Rejection or removal of submissions
  - Temporary or permanent suspension from submitting
- Repeated low-quality or bad-faith submissions may be treated as violations

**Final Note**
- Publishing on this platform is a privilege contingent on producing honest, thoughtful, and high-quality work
