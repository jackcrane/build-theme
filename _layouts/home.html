How can you submit work? Members of the SLU OSS community are encouraged to submit their work to Build@SLU OSS. While we enforce our quality standards, we do so in an open, transparent, and accessible manner.
  1. Submit a proposal of your work. This can be done by submitting an issue to the Build@SLU OSS GitHub repository with the tag "Proposal". Adherant to the issue template, you will include a one-line description (what will likely become your title), a draft abstract, and any information on the project you would like to share. The proposal process is not designed to block projects, but to ensure your work will match our standards. If it does not, the reviewers will provide suggestions on how you can improve your work so it more ready for submission.
  2. Write a draft of your publication. This is to be submitted in the form of a pull request to the Build@SLU OSS GitHub repository. Be sure to mention the proposal issue in the pull request. Include any graphics, data, code samples, or other supporting materials you intend to include in your publication.
  3. Peer reviews of your work. This is done by other members of the community. Anything involving your work is fair game for review. Reviewers are encouraged to provide constructive feedback regarding your problem, solution, implementation, and documentation. This is an iterative process, and you are encouraged to revise your work based on the feedback you receive. If you feel you have received feedback that you believe is not a progression to your work, you are encouraged to defend your work by clarifying in your publication.
  4. Professor/Expert Reviews of your work. This is mostly focused on semantics; ensuring your work is clear, your verbiage is professional, your citations are correct, and your writing is something Build@SLU OSS is ready to put it's name behind.
  5. Release. Once the PR is approved and merged, your work will immediately become available on the Build@SLU OSS website.

When submitting work, please be sure to adhere to the Build@SLU OSS policies and guidelines. Refer to the writing guide for documentation on using the rich text features of Build@SLU OSS. Always remember to be respectful and follow the rules outlined in the Codes of Conduct.
